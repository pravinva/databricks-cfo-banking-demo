Short Executive Talk Track

Deposit Modeling (60-75 seconds)

We run a three-layer deposit modeling stack so Treasury can move from static assumptions to behavior-driven stress projections.

  • Approach 1 establishes portfolio beta sensitivity by product and relationship segment.
  • Approach 2 adds vintage survival and runoff behavior, improving liquidity planning realism.
  • Approach 3 applies dynamic beta and regulatory stress views to quantify NII and EVE sensitivity under adverse paths.
  • Batch inference operationalizes the models for recurring portfolio-level decision support.

PPNR Scenario Planning (60-75 seconds)

PPNR planning extends beyond rate-only scenarios by integrating explicit market and liquidity shock channels for ALCO.

  • Train_PPNR_Models.py generates baseline Non-Interest Income, Non-Interest Expense, and monthly PPNR forecasts and registers UC model aliases.
  • Scenario planning introduces curve drivers and non-rate shocks including equity, credit spread, FX, and runoff.
  • NII repricing and ML inference refresh quarterly scenario outputs with auditable attribution.
  • Executive reporting and dashboard pages present Q1-Q9 trajectory, cumulative impact, and attribution decomposition.

Suggested Presentation Flow (5-7 minutes)

1) Start with deposit stability and risk sensitivity

Lead with headline balance-sheet stability before introducing model complexity. Anchor the opening in three numbers: total deposits, portfolio beta concentration, and projected runoff pressure.

  • Balance baseline: state current total deposits and whether trend is stable, expanding, or contracting over recent months.
  • Sensitivity posture: explain portfolio beta concentration by segment or product to show where repricing sensitivity is clustered.
  • Behavioral durability: use vintage and decay outputs to quantify stickiness versus runoff-prone balances.
  • Risk framing: distinguish structural runoff risk from cyclical pricing pressure so ALCO sees what is manageable through pricing versus what requires funding strategy.
  • Decision cue: close this section with which deposit cohorts require active monitoring in the next quarter.
2) Transition into stress behavior and planning horizon

Move from today’s position to forward-looking resilience. Show how balance-sheet behavior changes under stress and then connect that directly to quarterly earnings path impacts.

  • Model baseline first: reference Train_PPNR_Models.py outputs as the anchor baseline before scenario overlays.
  • Dynamic response: present dynamic beta behavior under rising and falling rate paths to demonstrate non-linear sensitivity.
  • Stress translation: summarize scenario outcomes for NII and EVE sensitivity, then bridge to PPNR trajectory impacts.
  • Scenario set: walk baseline, adverse rate, rate-cut, and market-shock scenarios with clear Q1, Q4, and Q9 checkpoints.
  • Planning horizon: explain why nine-quarter framing is used for governance and capital planning alignment.
  • Comparative insight: highlight where market and liquidity shocks produce materially different outcomes versus pure rate shocks.
3) Close with scenario governance and actionability

Finish with governance and execution: what is monitored, how often it is refreshed, and which channels treasury executives use for ongoing decision support.

  • Attribution discipline: quantify Q9 delta decomposition into rate, market, and liquidity components and confirm reconciliation to total delta.
  • Governance cadence: state model refresh rhythm, scenario rerun cadence, and control points for approval and rollback.
  • Operating model: point users to the AI/BI dashboard for structured monitoring and Genie for conversational follow-up questions.
  • Action register: define concrete actions by scenario state (pricing move, funding mix adjustment, hedging review, or escalation).
  • Executive close: summarize expected near-term posture and the trigger thresholds that would change strategy.
Presenter tip: keep commentary in business impact terms first, then explain model mechanics only where needed for confidence and governance.
